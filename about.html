What you need to know:

Ban on out of state money helps end forum shopping by groups that do not represent South Dakota and its citizens

Out of state groups donated $9 out of every $10 for 2016 ballot initiatives

A total of $11.1 million was raised to support/oppose ballot initiatives in 2016 - $9.5 million or 86% was from out-of-state interest

Marcy’s Law was solely funded by California Billionaire Henry T. Nicholas III - $2 million in total – Also funded ballot initiative campaigns in 6 other state in 2016

Select Management Resources LLC of Alpharetta, GA a pro-payday lending group donated more than $3 million to stop the cap of interest rates. Trying to trick the voter into passing conflicting laws.

Initiative and referendum process was created to protect South Dakota’s citizens from out of state influence. It is now being used by out of state interest to push an agenda not beneficial to the state’s voters.

Historically out of state interest have dominated the ballot initiative process for a multitude of reasons
  • - Second-lowest signature requirement in the country (Only North Dakota has lower requirement)
  • - Cheap media market to push their agenda
  • - No limits for PACs when contributing to ballot committees
